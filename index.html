Tribute to Mother Teresa

About Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa (1910–1997) was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary, widely admired for her lifelong dedication to helping the poor, sick, and dying. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in Kolkata, India, which grew into a global organization serving the most marginalized. Known for her humility, compassion, and selfless service, she received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. Mother Teresa’s life is remembered as a shining example of love, kindness, and unwavering commitment to humanity.

Achivements

  • In 1950, she founded the Missionaries of Charity in Kolkata.
  • The order started with 12 members but grew into thousands of sisters working in over 130 countries.
  • They run orphanages, schools, homes for the dying, leprosy clinics, and soup kitchens.
